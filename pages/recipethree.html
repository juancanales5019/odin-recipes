Carne en Vaho

What is Carne en Vaho?

This hearty dish features beef, plantains, and yuca, all slow-cooked together in layers. The result is a tender and flavorful meal often served during special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound beef brisket or flank steak, cut into large pieces
  • 1 pound yuca, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 3 green plantains, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 2 ripe plantains, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 2 tomatoes, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • Salt, pepper, and oregano to taste
  • Banana leaves

Steps

  1. Marinate the beef: Season the beef with garlic, vinegar, salt, pepper, and oregano. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour.
  2. Prepare the pot: Line the bottom of a large pot with banana leaves. Layer the ingredients in the pot, starting with the beef, followed by yuca, green plantains, ripe plantains, and slices of tomato, onion, and bell pepper.
  3. Steam the Carne en Vaho: Cover everything with banana leaves and add a small amount of water at the bottom of the pot. Cover tightly and cook on low heat for 3-4 hours until the meat is tender and the plantains and yuca are soft.
  4. Serve: Cut portions of the beef, yuca, and plantains. Serve with a side of cabbage salad and tortillas.
