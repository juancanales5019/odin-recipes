Nacatamales

What is a Nacatamal?

A traditional Nicaraguan tamale, Nacatamal is made from corn dough (masa) and filled with pork, rice, potatoes, and spices. It is wrapped in plantain leaves and steamed for several hours.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups masa (corn dough)
  • 1/2 pound pork, cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup uncooked rice
  • 1 potato, diced
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 2 tablespoons lard or butter
  • 1 tablespoon achiote (annatto)
  • Plantain leaves, softened in boiling water
  • Salt, pepper, and cumin to taste

Steps

  1. Prepare the masa: In a bowl, mix the masa with lard or butter, achiote, and a pinch of salt. Set aside.
  2. Cook the pork: Season the pork with salt, pepper, and cumin. Saut√© with minced garlic until lightly browned.
  3. Assemble the nacatamal: On a plantain leaf, spread a portion of masa. Add a spoonful of rice, a piece of pork, diced potatoes, slices of tomato, onion, and bell pepper. Fold the plantain leaf to wrap the tamal tightly.
  4. Steam the nacatamales: Place the wrapped nacatamales in a large pot with a small amount of water at the bottom. Cover and steam for 3-4 hours, adding water as necessary to avoid burning.
  5. Serve: Unwrap and enjoy hot, often served with coffee or a side of bread.
